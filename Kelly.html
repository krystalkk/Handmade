Don't expect a Pug to hunt, guard or retrieve. Pugs were bred to be companions, and that's exactly what they do best.
The Pug craves affection — and your lap — and is very unhappy if his devotion isn't reciprocated.
He tends to be a sedentary dog, content to sit in your lap as you read a book or watch a movie.
This doesn't mean the Pug is a stick-in-the-mud. Au contraire.
He is a playful, comical dog that enjoys living it up, and delights his owner with silly antics.
Always meet at least one of the parents — usually the mother is the one who's available —
to ensure that they have nice temperaments that you're comfortable with.
Meeting siblings or other relatives of the parents is also helpful for evaluating what a puppy will be like when he grows up.
Like every dog, the Pug needs early socialization — exposure to many different people, sights,sounds, and experiences — when they're young. Socialization helps ensure that your Pug puppy grows up to be a well-rounded dog.
